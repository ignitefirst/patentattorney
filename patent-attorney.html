FAQ

What is Business Planner?

A patent attorney provides technical, legal, and strategic expertise that dramatically increases your chances of success. Self-filed applications are often rejected or too weak to defend effectively.

How long does it take to get a patent?

While timelines vary, most utility patents take between one and three years depending on complexity, examiner workload, and required amendments.

Is a provisional patent enough to protect my idea?

A provisional application only establishes an initial filing date; it does not provide enforceable rights. A full non-provisional application must follow within 12 months.

Should I pursue trademarks or copyrights with my patent?

Often yes. Trademarks and copyrights protect branding and creative materials, adding layers of defense around your product and strengthening your market position.

What makes a strong patent strategy?

A strong strategy aligns your patent coverage with business goals, anticipates competitive threats, protects multiple aspects of your innovation, and evolves as your invention grows.
